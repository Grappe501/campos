# Volunteer Command — feed architecture

Feeds are how momentum becomes **visible**—not performative noise. The Movement Hub uses multiple feed **lanes** so volunteers get **official clarity**, **local energy**, and **personal relevance** without one undifferentiated firehose.

---

## Feed types

### Campaign feed

**Audience:** All volunteers (with role-appropriate filtering).

**Purpose:** **Rhythm of the campaign**—what matters this week, what changed, what success looks like. This is the closest thing to a **town square** in the Hub: leadership voice, comms-aligned updates, and selected operational truth.

**Content emphasis**

- **Selected official guidance** — priorities, message of the week, safety/legal reminders where appropriate.  
- **Action calls** — clear CTAs tied to tasks or events.  
- **Wins** — story-forward, values-aligned; scale-appropriate (not only huge metrics).  
- **Recruiting momentum** — Power of 5 culture framed as **invitation**, not pressure.  
- **Key events** — deadlines, trainings, big days—surfaced predictably.

**Tone:** Authoritative but **human**; calm; not endless ALL-CAPS urgency.

---

### Local feed

**Audience:** Volunteers scoped to a **geography** (county, city, precinct stack—per `group-architecture.md`).

**Purpose:** **Local organizing energy**—what’s happening on the ground, neighborhood-specific asks, local wins, local photos (where policy allows), and **local updates** that do not need to clutter the whole campaign.

**Content emphasis**

- Local **events** and **shifts** with clear logistics.  
- **Local updates**—turf coverage, weather-dependent changes, local partnerships.  
- **Photos** and **stories** that build pride (consent, dignity, no voter PII in images).  
- **Recognition** that makes local teams feel seen.

**Tone:** Neighborly; captains and local hosts set cultural temperature; moderation per `culture-and-moderation.md`.

---

### Personal or network feed

**Audience:** The signed-in volunteer.

**Purpose:** **Relevance**—what matters *to you* this week: your teams, your tasks, your invites, your buddies, your training due—**without** replacing the campaign’s official priorities.

**Content emphasis**

- Tasks, handoffs, and **nudges** (including Steve) framed as supportive, not nagging.  
- Activity from **groups you belong to** and **people you follow** (if follow exists), prioritized by **fit** over vanity.  
- **Recruiting momentum** personal—your invites, your follow-through prompts—without public shaming for low numbers.

**Tone:** Calm, adaptive; respects **paused** or low-capacity states.

**Guardrail:** This feed must not become a **shadow campaign feed** that contradicts official guidance—Steve and system rules **surface conflicts** to humans when detected.

---

### Group feed

**Audience:** Members of a specific group (event team, precinct crew, functional team, Power of 5 cluster, etc.).

**Purpose:** **Coordination and belonging** inside a defined container—where decentralized leadership does its daily work.

**Content emphasis**

- Logistics threads, quick questions, **wins**, **photos** from the work.  
- **Action calls** scoped to the group (“we need two more for Saturday”).  
- **Recognition** of individuals (opt-in visibility).  
- **Official guidance** when captains or staff pin posts—localized interpretation of campaign priorities.

**Tone:** Peer-heavy; moderators enforce norms; escalation for conflict.

---

## What belongs in feeds (cross-cutting)

These **content categories** can appear in more than one feed type; the **mixing rules** differ by surface.

| Category | Role |
|----------|------|
| **Events** | Time-bound; should always link to authoritative event objects (time, place, role). |
| **Wins** | Morale and story; avoid toxic comparison; celebrate effort and care. |
| **Recognition** | Public where appropriate; sensitive feedback stays private per doctrine. |
| **Action calls** | Clear CTA; honest time cost; no bait-and-switch asks. |
| **Local updates** | Ground truth for geography—weather, venue, partner constraints. |
| **Photos** | Consent, safety, compliance; no voter roll photos or sensitive identifiers. |
| **Recruiting momentum** | Relationship-first framing; Power of 5 as culture, not scoreboard. |
| **Selected official guidance** | Curated from leadership/comms; may appear in campaign + pinned in local/group as needed. |

---

## What should *not* dominate feeds

- Endless **metrics competitions** that shame low producers.  
- **Unvetted** rumors presented as fact—moderation and escalation apply.  
- **Staff chatter** that should live in Command Center, not volunteer town square—unless intentionally shared for transparency.

---

## Steve and feeds

- Steve may **summarize** “what matters for you” in the personal/network context—**transparent** about what it is doing.  
- Steve does **not** silently reorder campaign priorities; **official guidance** remains visible in campaign feed.  
- Sensitive contexts (conflict, safety) route to **humans**—see `culture-and-moderation.md` (Steve Layer).

---

## Relationship to messaging

- Feeds are **persistent, scan-friendly narrative** for a community.  
- **Direct and group messaging** handle fast logistics and private coaching (`network-layer.md`).  
- The product should avoid **five redundant channels** for the same announcement—**broadcast** and **pins** reduce duplication.

---

## Suggested phasing: V1 vs later

**V1**

- **Campaign feed** + **group feeds** for formed teams; **local feed** where geography groups exist.  
- Content types: **events**, **action calls**, **selected official guidance**, **wins** and **recognition** (moderated).  
- **Personal/network** as a **simple** chronological “for you” combining tasks + group highlights—minimal ranking.

**Later**

- Richer **media** policies and galleries; advanced **personalization**; cross-feed **digest** options; optional **follow** graph with strict privacy controls.
