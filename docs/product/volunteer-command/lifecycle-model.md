# Volunteer Command — lifecycle model

The lifecycle is the **choreography** Volunteer Command uses from first touch to multiplication. It is **not strictly linear**: volunteers loop back (e.g., re-training after rule changes), hold steady in a lane, or move faster on leadership tracks. Phases are **operational labels** for design and staffing—not moral judgments.

---

## Phase map (full lifecycle)

| Phase | Purpose | Primary owners | Typical outputs |
|-------|---------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Welcome** | Signal belonging; reduce anxiety; confirm identity and consent. | Steve + automated surfaces; humans on high-touch intakes | Welcomed volunteer; correct consent; clear “you’re in” moment |
| **Understand** | Build shared context: what the campaign is doing **this week**, how teams work, what lanes exist. | Steve (short), staff/captain briefings | Volunteer can state priorities in plain language; lane shortlist |
| **Assign** | Place the volunteer in a **lane** with a named scope, team anchor, and first task. | Staff/captain (guided/dual-track models); volunteer confirms fit | Assignment record; first task scheduled |
| **Support** | Sustain momentum: questions, logistics, emotional safety, routing to humans. | Captain, team feeds, Steve (light), staff escalation | Stalled states resolved; **needs follow-up** handled humanely |
| **Train** | Deliver **just-in-time** knowledge and compliance gates before sensitive actions. | Training modules; staff trainers; gated unlocks | Completions logged; restricted actions remain blocked until done |
| **Encourage** | Reinforce norms: celebrate publicly, correct privately; highlight care and consistency. | Team feeds, recognition surfaces, captains | Morale sustained without toxic comparison |
| **Expand** | Grow relational reach (Power of 5), deepen participation, optional new skills. | Volunteer + Steve prompts; captains; relational tools | Invites, retained new entrants, deeper lane skills |
| **Elevate** | Expand **scope** for those who want it—progressive leadership with permissions. | Staff + captains; leadership detection signals | Elevated role with training + audit |
| **Multiply** | Build capacity: others onboarded through this volunteer’s circle; succession. | Leaders + Volunteer Command; intake attribution | New volunteers in pipeline; healthy handoffs |

---

## Welcome

**Goal:** Move from “signed up” to **welcomed**—emotionally and operationally.

- **Includes:** confirmation message, Movement Hub entry, basic “what happens next,” duplicate merge if needed.  
- **Excludes:** dumping full policy manuals before context exists.  
- **Exit criteria:** Volunteer has identity, consent aligned to intended channels, and a visible next step within a short window.

---

## Understand

**Goal:** Shared mental model of the campaign’s current chapter—not generic ideology decks.

- **Includes:** weekly priorities, team structure, where to read updates, how to ask for help.  
- **Adaptive:** digital-only vs in-person emphasis; uncertainty path reduces density.  
- **Exit criteria:** Volunteer selects or accepts a lane direction (or explicitly defers with a scheduled check-in).

---

## Assign

**Goal:** **Fit**—lane, team, geography (if relevant), and first task with clear ownership.

- **Models:** guided (suggested placement), dual-track (relational + operational), self-directed with confirmation—see `behavior-model.md`.  
- **Exit criteria:** Assignment accepted **or** alternative lane chosen with **redirect** path; no silent discard.

---

## Support

**Goal:** Keep people from falling through cracks—logistics, clarity, safety concerns.

- **Includes:** office hours norms, captain response expectations, escalation to staff, **blocked** states for harassment or policy issues.  
- **Steve’s role:** light nudges, FAQs, routing—not therapy, not legal advice.  
- **Exit criteria:** Open questions resolved or ticketed to humans; **at-risk** states get human eyes.

---

## Train

**Goal:** Competence and compliance **when the action requires it**.

- **Includes:** micro-modules, refreshers before high-risk windows, leadership-specific training.  
- **Exit criteria:** Gates satisfied for the action class (e.g., voter contact, data access); **not** completion of irrelevant courses.

---

## Encourage

**Goal:** Culture reinforcement—**public celebration**, **private correction** where something is off.

- **Includes:** team shout-outs, milestone markers, optional visibility preferences.  
- **Guardrails:** no shame-based rankings; recognition tied to values in doctrine.

---

## Expand

**Goal:** Relationship-based growth and skill depth—without forcing everyone into leadership.

- **Includes:** Power of 5 prompts, follow-through with invited contacts, optional new lanes.  
- **Exit criteria:** Either expanded relational activity **or** explicit “steady lane” confirmation—both healthy.

---

## Elevate

**Goal:** **Progressive** leadership—scope increases with checkpoints, not charisma alone.

- **Includes:** captain path, moderator permissions, turf lead, training completions, staff confirmation for sensitive scope.  
- **Exit criteria:** Elevated role recorded; permissions and audit; **active leader** state where applicable.

---

## Multiply

**Goal:** **Capacity**—new volunteers brought in through trusted circles and properly onboarded.

- **Includes:** attribution, handoff to onboarding, quality of welcome for recruits (not just lead count).  
- **Exit criteria:** New people enter **welcome → understand** with integrity; multipliers get recognition for **retention**, not only invites.

---

## Loops and non-linearity

- **Re-training loop:** Compliance or tool change pulls someone back to **train** while still **active**.  
- **Reassignment loop:** **Support** → **assign** again when lane or life changes.  
- **Re-engagement loop:** **inactive** → **welcome** (light) → **understand** delta → **assign** new lane.  
- **Steady state:** A volunteer can remain **active** in one lane for a long time without entering **elevate**—success.

---

## Relationship to states

Lifecycle **phases** describe **what we are doing with someone**; the **state machine** (`state-machine.md`) describes **their status flag** for routing, automation, and dashboards. A volunteer can be **active** while cycling through **support** and **train** repeatedly.
